# My Preferences

## Work Style
- I do my sharpest thinking early — protect mornings for hard problems
- I tend to over-plan and under-execute; push me toward doing, not organizing
- I work better with longer, uninterrupted stretches than many small slots
- I prefer async communication; batching email/Slack to 2x per day
- Meetings before noon drain me — schedule them in the afternoon when possible

## Day Structure
- 6:00 AM: Wake up, coffee, 20-minute walk
- 6:30–8:30 AM: Deep work block (best focus of the day — protect this)
- 8:30–9:00 AM: Breakfast, news, slow transition
- 9:00 AM–12:00 PM: Secondary focus block; okay for calls after 10 AM
- 12:00–1:00 PM: Lunch break — step away from screens
- 1:00–4:00 PM: Admin, meetings, lighter work
- 4:00–5:00 PM: Wind-down; review tomorrow, clear inbox
- Evenings: personal time only — no work after 6 PM

## Task Preferences
- Prefer to tackle the hardest task first thing (eat the frog)
- I tend to accumulate too many "someday" tasks — prune ruthlessly
- No more than 1 major creative/strategic task per day; they take full energy
- Always flag tasks that have been sitting open more than 2 weeks
- I respond better to "do X for 30 min" than open-ended tasks

## A Good Day Looks Like
- Made real progress on the hardest thing, not just the easy things
- Had a real lunch break
- Inbox at zero by 4 PM
- Learned something new
- Moved my body at some point

## Stress Signals & Reset
- Warning signs: jumping between tabs constantly, rewriting the same sentence, skipping meals
- Reset: close everything, walk around the block, come back with one specific next action

## Reminders
- Hard problems first. Admin second.
- If it takes less than 2 minutes, do it now.
- Step outside at some point — even 10 minutes helps.

## Podcast Preferences
- Busy day (>= 3 hrs of meetings): prefer narrative podcasts — storytelling, history, true crime
- Open day (< 3 hrs of meetings): prefer interviews and ideas — tech, science, business
